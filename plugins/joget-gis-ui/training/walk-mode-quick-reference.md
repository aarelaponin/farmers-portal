# Walk Mode — Quick Reference Card

**For field workers capturing boundaries on a mobile device**

---

## Before You Start

- Be at the location with your mobile phone
- Make sure GPS (Location) is turned ON in your phone settings
- Stand in an open area away from buildings and trees for best GPS signal

---

## Steps

### 1. Open the Form

Open the form that has the map. You will see the map area.

### 2. Tap "Capture Boundary"

Tap the **Capture Boundary** button to begin.

### 3. Choose "Walk the Boundary"

If you see two options, tap **Walk the Boundary**.

### 4. Wait for GPS Signal

Look at the GPS panel in the top-right corner. It shows your GPS accuracy.

| Bar Color | Accuracy | What It Means            |
|-----------|----------|--------------------------|
| Green     | ±3m or less  | **Excellent** — Ready to mark |
| Green     | ±5m or less  | **Good** — Ready to mark      |
| Yellow    | ±10m or less | **Fair** — Wait if you can     |
| Orange    | ±20m or less | **Poor** — Move to open area   |
| Red       | Over ±20m    | **Very poor** — Check GPS      |

**Wait until the bar is green or yellow before you start marking.**

### 5. Walk to the First Corner

Go to a corner of the boundary. Stand still for a moment.

### 6. Tap "Mark Corner"

Tap the big **Mark Corner** button at the bottom of the screen.

You will feel a vibration and see a message: **"Corner 1 marked"**.

### 7. Walk to the Next Corner

Walk along the boundary edge to the next corner. Tap **Mark Corner** again.

### 8. Keep Going

Repeat for every corner of the boundary. Walk in order around the whole boundary.

> You need **at least 3 corners** to make a boundary.

### 9. Close the Boundary

When you walk back near your starting point, a yellow message appears:

**"Close to start point. Close Polygon"**

Tap the **Close Polygon** button.

### 10. Review

Check the boundary on the map. The panel at the bottom shows:

- **Area** — size in hectares
- **Perimeter** — distance around in meters
- **Corners** — number of corners marked
- **GPS Avg** — average GPS accuracy

### 11. Save

If the boundary looks correct, save the form.

If you want to start over, tap **Redraw**.

---

## Common Problems

| Problem | What to Do |
|---------|------------|
| "Mark Corner" button is grey | GPS accuracy is too low. Move to an open area and wait. |
| GPS bar stays red | Check that GPS is turned ON. Move away from buildings and trees. |
| "Boundary lines are crossing" | Some lines overlap. Tap **Redraw** and try again. Walk the corners in order. |
| Overlap warning appears | Your boundary touches another boundary. Tap **Adjust Boundary** to fix, or **Save Anyway** if it is correct. |
| "No internet connection" | The map may not load new tiles, but you can still mark corners. |

---

## Tips

- Walk **slowly** at each corner — stand still when you tap Mark Corner
- Walk the boundary in **one direction** (all clockwise or all counter-clockwise)
- Mark corners where the boundary **changes direction**
- Straight edges need only a corner at **each end**
- More corners on curved edges gives a better shape
- If you make a mistake, tap **Redraw** and start over
